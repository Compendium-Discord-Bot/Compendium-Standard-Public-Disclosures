# Community Guidelines and Content Standards

## I. Purpose

Compendium exists to help people create, organize, and share fictional worlds, campaigns, characters, and stories. These Guidelines are intended to preserve a welcoming environment for creators while maintaining clear boundaries regarding content and behavior.

These Guidelines supplement our [Terms of Service](Terms-of-Service.md "Terms of Service – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures"). Violations of these Guidelines are enforced under our [Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy](Safety-Enforcement-and-Appeals-Policy.md "Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures").

---

## II. Guiding Principles

As an organizational and storytelling tool, our mission is to support creative expression without dictating artistic, literary, or moral viewpoints. Our purpose is to enable storytelling by enriching your storytelling experience by taking out much of the busywork of arranging your world, while still offering finely-tuned controls over your work.

The Service does not prohibit themes merely because they are controversial, uncomfortable, or depict conflict, vice, tragedy, or other mature subject matter.

Our standards focus primarily on:

* User safety;
* Respectful participation;
* Levels of depiction;
* Appropriate use of the Service.

In line with our mission, we place boundaries on uses of the Service which stray outside of our mission's purpose.

---

## III. Community Conduct

Users may not use Compendium to:

* Harass, threaten, or intimidate others.
* Engage in targeted abuse or hate-based conduct.
* Store or distribute real world identities or information about non-public persons without their knowledge and consent, such as names, contact information, addresses, or government identification numbers.
* Store or distribute classified materials or patented intellectual property for which they do not have the right to store or distribute.
* Impersonate others for deceptive purposes.
* Spam, disrupt, or intentionally degrade the Service.
* Encourage, participate in or aid in the concealment of unlawful activity.

Disagreements regarding fictional settings, themes, or creative choices are not, by themselves, violations of these Guidelines.

## IV. Content Ratings

### 1. How content ratings work

Compendium tags every document with a content rating to prevent exposure of mature material to audiences who have not opted into it. Rather than a single guild-wide setting, the rating is carried by the **template** a document is built from, and in some cases by the **document** itself. The rating field has three values: **`SFW`**, **`Possible-NSFW`**, and **`NSFW`** ("Not Safe For Work"), defaulting to **`SFW`**. `NSFW`-eligible content is explained in more detail in **§IV(4)** below; content that is prohibited outright, regardless of tag, is explained in **§IV(4)(C)**.

### 2. Template Behavior

* **`SFW` templates** lock every document created from them to `SFW`. No document under an `SFW` template may be marked otherwise.
* **`NSFW` templates** automatically tag every document created from them as `NSFW`.
* **`Possible-NSFW` templates** are discretionary: the author tags each document themselves at creation. When a document is submitted for acceptance into a guild, the reviewing guild staff may, at their discretion, override the author's tag before accepting it.

### 3. Display Restrictions

Documents tagged `NSFW` may only be displayed:

* As an ephemeral message visible only to the document's own creator;
* In a direct message; or
* In a Discord channel that has been [marked as NSFW](https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/115000084051-Age-Restricted-Channels-and-Content "Age-Restricted Channels and Content – Discord").

Compendium will not post `NSFW` content into any other channel or message context, regardless of guild settings.

### 4. Content rating list

The core test for what qualifies as `NSFW` versus what is prohibited outright: content that **references or implies** a mature theme (including fade-to-black treatment) remains eligible for `NSFW`. Content that **depicts or details** a mature theme graphically is prohibited outright, regardless of how it is tagged - see **§IV(4)(C)** below.

#### A. SFW

Suitable for general audiences.

Examples include:

* Fantasy adventures;
* Historical settings;
* Non-graphic violence;
* Romance;
* Political intrigue.

#### B. NSFW (Mature, permitted when tagged and gated)

May include, when referenced or implied rather than graphically depicted:

* Horror;
* War;
* Death and grief;
* Crime;
* Moral ambiguity;
* References to sexuality or prostitution;
* Non-graphic portrayals of violence;
* References to torture or abuse;
* Romantic relationships and implied intimacy.

#### C. Prohibited [Explicit] Content

As a storytelling platform, we choose not to host or deliver explicit content on our platform, as we believe that the mission of explicit content has separate goals beyond storytelling. While we value creative freedom, we do not permit the following content on Compendium in any form, regardless of tag, guild, or channel:

* Explicit sexual content;
* Pornographic content;
* Erotic roleplay archives;
* Sexualized depictions of minors or characters who plausibly appear to be minors;
* Content depicting or promoting child exploitation;
* Non-consensual sexual content intended primarily for titillation;
* Illegal content;
* Content intended primarily to shock through gratuitous graphic depictions.

### 5. Mistagging

Tagging `NSFW`-appropriate content as `SFW` is a violation of these Guidelines, independent of whether the content itself was permitted. Mistagging is subject to enforcement under our [Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy](Safety-Enforcement-and-Appeals-Policy.md "Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures"), scaled to severity and repetition.

## V. Mature Themes and Depiction Standards

The Service permits mature themes. The existence of a topic in a fictional world does not, by itself, violate these Guidelines.

For example, fictional settings may include:

* Brothels;
* Criminal organizations;
* Slavery;
* Religious conflict;
* War;
* Tragedy;
* Romance;
* Vice and corruption.

However, content should generally present these subjects in service of:

* Storytelling;
* Worldbuilding;
* Characterization;
* Gameplay.

Content whose primary purpose is sexual stimulation or graphic spectacle is not permitted.

---

## VI. Sexual Content

The following are generally permissible:

* References to romantic relationships;
* References to sexual relationships;
* Implied intimacy;
* Fade-to-black depictions;
* Character histories involving relationships or vice, or simple references to present vices.

The following are prohibited:

* Detailed descriptions of sexual acts;
* Detailed erotic fiction;
* Pornographic illustrations;
* Explicit depictions of intimacy.
* Any involvement of a minor character in the above prohibited items.

---

## VII. Images and Illustrations

Images should support storytelling and worldbuilding, not sexual stimulation.

Illustrations may depict:

* Fantasy costumes;
* Historical attire;
* Artistic nudity where context clearly supports a narrative or artistic purpose.

The following are prohibited:

* Pornographic imagery;
* Explicit sexual acts;
* Sexualized depictions of minors or characters whose apparent age is ambiguous.

When context and presentation create substantial uncertainty regarding whether a character may reasonably be perceived as underage, the Service may remove the content to protect users and the integrity of the platform, and may impose additional penalties.

---

## VIII. Moderator Discretion

No policy can anticipate every circumstance.

The Service reserves the right to evaluate content in context, including:

* Accompanying text;
* Presentation;
* Pattern of conduct;
* Apparent purpose of the material.

Moderation decisions are guided by the principles expressed in these Guidelines and are not intended to impose editorial viewpoints regarding fictional subject matter.

---

## IX. Enforcement

Violations of these Guidelines are enforced under our [Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy](Safety-Enforcement-and-Appeals-Policy.md "Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures"), which describes the full catalog of enforcement actions and how severity is assessed.

In cases of illegality, we report the misconduct to the relevant authorities.

---

## X. Appeals

Users may appeal moderation actions through the procedures described in our [Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy](Safety-Enforcement-and-Appeals-Policy.md "Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures").

---

## XI. Final Principle

We are in the business of helping people tell stories.

We do not seek to determine which stories are morally acceptable. We ask only that those stories be shared with restraint, discretion, and respect for the boundaries established by these Guidelines.
