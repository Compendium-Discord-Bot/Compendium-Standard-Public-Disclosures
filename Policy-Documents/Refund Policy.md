# Compendium Refund Policy

## 0. Disclaimers

We currently offer no paid services as this time. All of the below reflects future policy transparency efforts and may be subject to change. As always, we will commit any updates to the [#transparency-hub](https://discord.com/channels/1232458290563387392/1345476136867594250 "Compendium Support Hub – Transparency Hub") channel in our Support Server on Discord.

Nothing in this Refund Policy limits or excludes any rights that you may have under applicable consumer protection laws. Where local law grants you rights that are more favorable than this policy, those rights will apply.

## I. Summary

We at Compendium stand by our commitment to provide a great product. If you are dissatisfied with what we have provided, we invite you to please seek a refund quickly and tell us how we can improve!

The Policy describes guarantees to you as a customer of our refund commitments, and provides methods required to remediate any issues.

## II. Eligibility & Limitations

### 1. Memberships

In order to be eligible for a refund, you must be an existing or former subscriber to any premium subscription tiers and must contact us through valid channels, See **§III Process, ¶ 1. Acceptable Processes**.

### 2. Timeline Eligibility

1. Refund requests submitted within 40 days of any purchase are elligible for refund to original tender.
2. Refund requests submitted from within 41 to 90 days of any purchase are elligible for issuance of credit to your existing Compendium account.
3. Refund requests submitted in excess of 90 days are eligible for neither refund nor credit.

### 3. Refund Limitations

Because we stand by our product, we limit refund eligibility to include no more than 5 transactions in a 365-day timespan, and 7 transactions over your entire subscription history. We welcome feedback on how we can improve first.

### 4. Credit as Original Tender

If credit issued by us was used as a payment method for a transaction, whether in whole or in part, the amount originally paid as credit will be re-issued as credit.

For more information on what credit is and how we handle it, see our [Credit Policy](Credit-Policy.md "Credit Policy – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures").

### 5. Promotions & Discounts

The original, higher value of the discounted item will not be refunded. Only the amount paid not by credit will be.

When issueing a refund, we presume that you are dissatisfied with Compenium or it has fallen into disuse for you. As a result, of seeking a refund, any live subscription will also be cancelled.

1. Contact us in our Support Server using the Billing Ticket template as the user linked to the premium subscription.
2. Contact us via our email at support@`[WIP - Website domain]` including the following:
   1. Your intent in seeking a refund;
   2. The Discord User ID for the user linked to the subscription;
   3. All related Invoice IDs which you are using to seek refunds;
   4. `[WIP - Some other identifier to prove who you say you are.]`

2. We ask for reasonable notice to resolve your issue. As such, we request that you make the effort to follow one of the acceptable processes above before seeking a chargeback (AKA card dispute, bank dispute, charge dispute, etc.), which are not the same as refunds. Chargebacks incur fees against us from our financial institutions and cause reputational harm with them. We consider chargebacks initiated without first attempting to resolve the issue through our refund process to be abusive. Certain actions, including account terminations arising from fraudulent conduct, may be final and therefore not be eligible for appeal.
   1. You submitted only one chargeback for a Compendium transaction to your bank or payment processor, ever, AND;
   2. You have submitted a valid refund request by either of the above processes in **¶ 1. Acceptable Processes** above, and have not received a statement from us within 30 days, attesting that your refund has been processed.

### 3. Chargeback Exceptions

Chargebacks initiated without first attempting to resolve the matter through our published refund process may be treated as abuse of our payment systems and may result in account restrictions or termination.

This provision does not apply where the customer reasonably believes that the transaction was unauthorized, fraudulent, or otherwise requires immediate action through their financial institution.

The Service reserves the right, but not the obligation, to review requests for reinstatement arising from exceptional circumstances.

## IV. Refund Inelligibility

Under extreme circumstances, users may void their ability to receive a refund.

Examples of cases where refunds will not be tendered:

* A user has executed a chargeback.
* A user has used all of their refunds
* We may review account activity and service usage to detect refund abuse, fraud, or circumvention of our policies. Any users engaged in fraudulent activity may receive unique penalties, such as ineligibility for subscriptions, or ineligibility for refunds, and general penalties up to and including a permanent ban from Compendium.
