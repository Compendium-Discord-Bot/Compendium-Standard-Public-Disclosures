# Safety, Enforcement and Appeals Policy

## I. Purpose

This Policy explains how the Service investigates reports, enforces its [Community Guidelines and Content Standards](Community-Guidelines-and-Content-Standards.md "Community Guidelines and Content Standards – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures"), and reviews appeals of moderation actions.

The goal of Trust & Safety is to preserve a safe and welcoming environment for creators while applying our rules fairly, consistently, and transparently.

This Policy supplements our [Terms of Service](Terms-of-Service.md "Terms of Service – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures") and [Community Guidelines and Content Standards](Community-Guidelines-and-Content-Standards.md "Community Guidelines and Content Standards – Compendium Standard Public Disclosures").

---

## II. Guiding Principles

Our moderation practices are guided by the following principles:

* **Safety** – Protect users and the integrity of the Service.
* **Fairness** – Evaluate reports based upon evidence and context.
* **Proportionality** – Take actions that are appropriate to the nature and severity of a violation.
* **Consistency** – Apply standards uniformly regardless of a user's popularity, status, or opinions.
* **Good Faith** – Recognize that mistakes, misunderstandings, and differences in creative expression may occur.

---

## III. Reporting Violations

Users may report:

* Content that violates our policies;
* Harassment or abuse;
* Spam or disruptive conduct;
* Illegal content;
* Impersonation or fraud;
* Suspected account compromise.

Reports should include, where possible:

* A link to the content;
* Screenshots or evidence;
* A description of the concern.

Knowingly submitting false or malicious reports may itself constitute a violation of our policies.

---

## III. Investigation Process

Upon receiving a report, the Service may:

1. Review the reported content;
2. Review surrounding context, including other content and metadata not immediately related to the reported content;
3. Temporarily flag elements of the surrounding context to be monitored to establish sufficient surrounding context;
4. Review prior conduct or repeated violations;
5. Seek additional information from involved users;
6. Temporarily restrict content while an investigation is ongoing.

Not every report results in enforcement action.

The Service is not obligated to act solely because content is controversial, unpopular, or offensive to particular viewpoints.

---

## IV. Moderator Evaluation

Moderators evaluate content in context.

Factors that may be considered include:

* The apparent purpose of the content;
* Whether the content serves storytelling or worldbuilding purposes;
* The degree of explicitness or graphic detail;
* Whether the content poses a risk to users or the Service;
* Patterns of repeated conduct;
* Report volume, attesting to the scale of impact on the community.

No single factor is necessarily determinative.

---

## V. Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity and context, the Service may take one or more of the following actions.

### 1. Formal Warning

A violation is recorded.

Repeated warnings may result in additional enforcement.

---

### 2. Content Removal

Specific content may be removed or hidden.

---

### 3. Feature Restrictions

Certain capabilities may be temporarily limited, including:

* Image uploads;
* Public publishing;
* Guild administration;
* Sharing features;
* Import or export features;
* Knowledge management features.

---

### 4. Temporary Suspension

Access to some or all of the Service is suspended for a defined period.

---

### 5. Premium Subscription Ban

Accounts or payment methods which interact with the Premium component of the Service in an exploitative, fraudulent, or predatory fashion may be prohibited from enhancing guilds with a Premium subscription.

---

### 6. Permanent Account Termination

Accounts that repeatedly or severely violate our policies may be permanently removed from the Service.

---

### 7. Emergency Actions

The Service may immediately disable content or accounts when reasonably necessary to:

* Protect users;
* Comply with legal obligations;
* Prevent abuse;
* Address credible threats;
* Protect the integrity of the platform.

Emergency actions may occur before notice is provided.

### 8. Breaches of the Law

As a mandatory reporter, if the Service finds that accounts use the Service in furtherance of a crime, the Service may report violations to the relevant authorities for the violation.

---

## VI. Progressive Enforcement

Whenever practical, enforcement generally follows an escalating model:

1. Informal Guidance;
2. Educational Warning;
3. Formal Warning;
4. Temporary Restrictions;
5. Suspension;
6. Termination.

However, severe violations may result in immediate suspension or termination.

---

## VII. Repeat Violations

The Service may examine or consider a user's prior conduct when determining enforcement actions.

Repeated violations may result in increasingly severe consequences, even if individual violations would otherwise warrant lesser action.

For the purposes of appeals, we define one or more violations that arise from the same conduct, series of related acts, or ongoing pattern of substantially similar behavior as **Related Enforcement Matter**. Violations discovered at any time may be attributed to the same Related Enforcement Matter.

---

## VIII. Content Preservation

Removed content may be retained for a reasonable period for:

* Appeals;
* Investigations;
* Security;
* Compliance with legal obligations.

The Service is not obligated to restore content that violates our policies.

---

# IX. Appeals and Finality of Decisions

Users may request reconsideration of moderation actions unless the action is expressly designated as final or ineligible for review.

Submission of an appeal does not guarantee that a substantive review will occur.

The Service may decline to review appeals involving:

* Severe or egregious policy violations;
* Child safety matters;
* Illegal content;
* Ban evasion;
* Fraudulent conduct;
* Unauthorized degradation to the Service, or any attempts to compromise its security interests;
* Repeated or abusive appeals;
* Circumstances where legal, safety, or operational considerations require finality.

When an appeal is accepted for review, the Service may:

* Uphold the action;
* Modify the action;
* Reverse the action;
* Request additional information.

Unless otherwise stated, users are generally limited to one appeal per Related Enforcement Matter.

After review, the Service's decision is final.

The Service reserves the right to reopen a matter at its sole discretion if new evidence becomes available or if an error is discovered.

---

## X. Appeal Review

Appeals are reviewed independently when reasonably practicable.

The reviewer may:

* Uphold the action;
* Modify the action;
* Reverse the action;
* Request additional information.

If more violations are discovered during the appeal review process relevant to the Related Enforcement Matter that were not previously known or where penalties were not applied, these violations will be associated with the same Related Enforcement Matter, and the same appeal. Pursuant to § IV. , reviewers may determine that the penalties imposed for the Related Enforcement Matter were insufficient, and may impose additional penalties.

Appeal decisions are final unless the Service elects to conduct an additional review.

The Service reserves the right to maintain documentation concerning any findings in the appeal process, regardless of the outcome.

---

## XI. Restoration Following Appeal

If an appeal is successful, the Service may:

* Restore content;
* Reinstate accounts;
* Remove strikes or warnings;
* Reverse restrictions.

The Service may decline to restore content that creates legal, technical, or security concerns.

---

## XII. Transparency

The Service may provide information regarding:

* The nature of a violation;
* The policy involved;
* The action taken.

To protect users and platform security, the Service may withhold:

* Internal moderation procedures;
* Detection methods;
* Reporter identities;
* Information that could facilitate abuse or evasion.

---

## XIII. Abuse of Moderation Systems

The following activities may themselves constitute violations:

* Filing knowingly false reports;
* Harassing moderators;
* Attempting to evade enforcement;
* Creating accounts to circumvent restrictions;
* Coordinated abuse of reporting systems.

---

## XIV. Creative Expression and Editorial Neutrality

The Service hosts a wide range of fictional worlds and viewpoints.

Moderation decisions are intended to enforce platform rules and protect users, not to endorse or reject particular creative, political, philosophical, religious, or artistic perspectives.

The existence of mature, controversial, or morally complex subject matter in a fictional work does not, by itself, constitute a policy violation.

---

## XV. Final Principle

Trust & Safety exists to preserve the ability of people to create and share stories responsibly.

We seek to exercise moderation with restraint, fairness, and transparency, while recognizing that no policy can anticipate every circumstance or eliminate the need for human judgment.
